Connecting Women in Need to Credible Support Groups

The Endometriosis Network Redesign

To connect women in need to credible support groups through a . non profit website redesign.

Summarizing
What is Endometriosis?
Endometriosis is a condition where tissue like the uterus lining grows outside it. This tissue behaves similarly to the uterine lining, breaking down and bleeding during the menstrual cycle. However, as it's outside the uterus, it can't exit the body, causing pain, inflammation, and possible fertility issues. This leads to discomfort to those affected during periods and beyond.
Project Overview
Our team was tasked to offer a not for profit organization a new website design that would improve their user experience, retention and call to actions. We chose The Endometriosis Network of Canada as a prime candidate as the current design had potential to simplify information architecture,
My Role as a UX/UI designer
My major roles focused user research scripting, user interviews, analyzing data and prioritization.
Scope
3 Weeks with a 4 person team
Limitations
No stakeholder feedback and short timeline
Tools
Zoom, Figma, Optimal Workshop, Trello, Google Survey, Facebook Groups
Deliverables
Heuristic evaluation, User persona, Lo-fi and Hi-fi Fidelity Desktop Mockups, Affinity Diagram, Card sort, Style Guide
Researching
Analyzing the Current State
To understand the current issues and key points to test, we conducted a heuristic analysis of the current state of the website.
Setting our Objectives to Understand our User
Our user research plan set out to explore these three objectives.
  • What do users expect from non profit organizations?
  • What are the needs of a user with Endometriosis?
  • What are the habits of a user who wants to support a loved one with an illness?
Conducting our Research
We decided on two main methods to understand our user base, one on one interviews and a survey. Interviews allow for the most indepth and insightful responses, however due to the nature of the illness and difficulty in finding participants who are willing to disclose such personal matters, we opted to have a survey posted to endometriosis support circles on facebook and other relevant forums.
  • Interviews- 4 one on one interviews with endometriosis users or non profit affiliated users
  • Survey- 26 participants found via endometriosis support facebook groups
Discovering the Key Findings
We found three main takeaways from our User Research.
  • Lack of information
  • Lack of support
  • Difficulty in finding support groups
Defining
Defining the Average User
To understand the user base for The Endometriosis Network , we analyzed our affinity diagram using our interview data to create an average user.
Defining the Problem
Dismissed Diana needs a credible place to find support because of feeling dismissed by multiple physicians and is not able to find good resources or support groups through online research and/or doctors.
Testing and Prioritizing our Design Changes
We conducted 4 guerrilla tests on the current state of website to define the redesign priorities for our users. We found three main priorities heard from users.
  • “Is this for me?” Brand Confusion
  • Information Overload and Navigation Overlap
  • Inconsistent Colour Symbolism
Receiving direct user input through Card Sorting
To understand the most intuitive navigation groupings, we got 6 participants to card sort the all primary and secondary terms through Optimal Workshop. This influenced our final information architecture of the site when reviewing the standardization grid results.
Ideating
Creating a Style Guide
To gain trust with users we created a style guide that is a softer more welcoming colour scheme that is more inclusive to the female demographic while still being true to the symbolic colours of endometriosis of salmon pink and yellow.
Building Lo-Fi Mockups
We built lo-fi mockups prioritizing fixing the issues in our guerrilla tests, with the short scope we wanted to focus on the most impactful changes.
